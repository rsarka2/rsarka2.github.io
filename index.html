About Me

Hi! My name is Rohan Sarkar, and I am a second-year undergraduate at Boston University studying Biomedical Engineering with a concentration in Nanotechnology and a minor in Chemistry, as part of the Kilachand Honors College. I am especially interested in drug delivery, nanomedicine, and AI-powered clinical tools, where engineering and biology intersect to directly impact patient outcomes.

Through research and professional experience, I have worked on antibody-functionalized nanoparticles for targeted cancer therapy, AI document classification systems for clinical data, and embedded systems for accessibility. I am also currently developing a 3D-printed bionic hand prototype, exploring how mechanical design and iterative CAD modeling can restore natural motion. I enjoy tackling problems that sit at the boundary of multiple disciplines, whether in the wet lab, writing code, or designing hardware. I am currently a Software Engineer at CareGP in Sydney, Australia, and I am always looking to bring my interdisciplinary background to projects that matter.
